Policy Renewal Guide - How to Renew Your Insurance Policy
This document explains the policy renewal process, important timelines, and tips to ensure continuous coverage without any gaps.

Why Renewal is Important:
- Ensures continuous coverage without gaps
- Maintains no-claim bonus benefits
- Avoids re-application and medical tests in some cases
- Protects accumulated benefits and waiting period credits

Renewal Timeline:
- Renewal notices are sent 45-60 days before expiry
- Grace period of 15-30 days is provided after expiry
- Renewing after grace period may require fresh underwriting
- Coverage lapses if not renewed within grace period

Steps to Renew:
1. Review your current policy coverage and assess if adequate
2. Compare premiums with other insurers if needed
3. Pay the renewal premium before the due date
4. Update any changes in personal information
5. Receive renewed policy document within 7 working days

No-Claim Bonus (NCB):
- Discount offered for not making claims during policy year
- NCB ranges from 5% to 50% depending on claim-free years
- NCB is transferable when switching insurers
- NCB is lost if a claim is made during the policy year

Tips for Renewal:
- Always renew before expiry to avoid coverage gaps
- Review and update sum insured to match current needs
- Consider adding riders or add-ons during renewal
- Check if any waiting periods reset on policy lapse

Validity: Renewed annually or as per original policy term
