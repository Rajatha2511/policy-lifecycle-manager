Common Policy Exclusions - What Insurance Does Not Cover
This document explains the most common exclusions found across different types of insurance policies that policyholders should be aware of.

General Exclusions (Apply to Most Policies):
- Intentional self-inflicted injuries or damage
- Loss or damage due to war, invasion, or civil unrest
- Nuclear, biological, or chemical contamination
- Damage caused while under influence of alcohol or drugs
- Illegal activities or criminal acts

Health Insurance Exclusions:
- Pre-existing conditions during waiting period (usually 2-4 years)
- Cosmetic or aesthetic treatments
- Experimental or unproven medical treatments
- Infertility treatments unless specifically covered
- Obesity treatment and weight loss surgery

Vehicle Insurance Exclusions:
- Normal wear and tear of vehicle parts
- Mechanical or electrical breakdown
- Damage when driving without valid license
- Using personal vehicle for commercial purposes
- Consequential losses

Home Insurance Exclusions:
- Gradual deterioration or wear and tear
- Damage caused by pests, insects, or vermin
- Loss of cash or valuables above specified limits
- Damage due to construction or renovation work

Life Insurance Exclusions:
- Death by suicide within first two years of policy
- Death due to participation in hazardous activities
- Death caused by pre-existing undisclosed conditions
- Death while committing a criminal act

Important Note:
Always read the policy document carefully to understand specific exclusions. Some exclusions can be removed by paying additional premium for add-on covers.
